## How to Stake SOL <a href="#h_01hc88s1afqv7a1bn9pswhpp90" id="h_01hc88s1afqv7a1bn9pswhpp90"></a>

### 1. Create or Restore Compatible Wallet <a href="#h_01hc88s1affgbfr9tnr8pzeacz" id="h_01hc88s1affgbfr9tnr8pzeacz"></a>

For this guide, we recommend using [Phantom](https://phantom.app/) or [Solflare](https://solflare.com/) wallets.&#x20;

* For the process demonstration of this guide, we will be showing Phantom, however, the steps will be quite similar using Solflare as well.

{% hint style="success" %}
If you've already got a compatible wallet, feel free to skip ahead to [#h\_01hc88s1agns5dzhzr93848rb6](outdated-how-to-stake-sol-solana.md#h_01hc88s1agns5dzhzr93848rb6 "mention")
{% endhint %}

{% hint style="warning" %}
If you don't already have Phantom or Solflare installed, please see the sections below for a quick start guide for each respective wallet.&#x20;

**For Phantom:** [getting-started-phantom-wallet.md](../../wallets/wallets/getting-started-phantom-wallet.md "mention")

**For Solflare:** [getting-started-solflare-wallet.md](../../wallets/wallets/getting-started-solflare-wallet.md "mention")
{% endhint %}

{% hint style="info" %}
If you're using the browser extension version of either wallet it can be convenient to pin it to your browser toolbar by clicking on the puzzle piece 🧩 icon and then the pin :pushpin: icon to pin it to your browser's extension bar.
{% endhint %}

***

### 2. Logging in to your Wallet <a href="#h_01hc88s1agns5dzhzr93848rb6" id="h_01hc88s1agns5dzhzr93848rb6"></a>

Once you have funded your Phantom wallet with Solana, you can click on the Phantom extension to see your account details.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (62).png" alt="" width="352"><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

### 3. Stake <a href="#h_01hc88s1agewspz738cwtyy6t1" id="h_01hc88s1agewspz738cwtyy6t1"></a>

Once logged in, you will be able to view all the SPL assets that you possess. If you do not already have SOL tokens you can get them from a friend or buy them from and exchange or other trusted third-party and transfer them to your wallet.

Once you do that you will be able to see the SOL tokens in your wallet.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (63).png" alt="" width="375"><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

Go ahead and click your SOL balance and you will see 3 dots and the "More" button. Click the button to reveal the staking menu. Click the menu-item "Stake SOL"

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-05-20 at 6.13.24 PM.png" alt="" width="358"><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

You will be shown a list of validators along with a search button.&#x20;

Go ahead and search for Chorus One in the search panel.&#x20;

{% hint style="warning" %}
A few validator's managed by Chorus One will likely show up. It is advisable to stake to the **main Chorus One validator** as shown in the screenshot below.
{% endhint %}

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2025-02-18 at 10.12.38 AM.png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

Choose the amount of SOL you would like to stake and click on Stake.

{% hint style="info" %}
Make sure to leave some SOL in your account to pay for the transaction fees.
{% endhint %}

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (65).png" alt="" width="350"><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

Once you click Stake you will immediately see that your wallet is staking your SOL to the Chorus One validator..&#x20;

You can also click on the 'View Transaction' link to see the status of your transaction on a block explorer.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (66).png" alt="" width="375"><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

If you look at the confirmations field you can slowly see it increasing from 0 to 32.&#x20;

Once it reaches the MAX number of confirmations your transaction gets added to the blockchain.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (67).png" alt=""><figcaption><p>Block explorer view of the Solana transaction status</p></figcaption></figure>

Make sure to note down the transaction hash or the link provided on the screen. This allows for easier debugging in case of a failed transaction.

After your transaction is successful you can go back to your Phantom wallet, click on the Solana balance, and see your **stake accounts.**

{% hint style="success" %}
**Congratulations! You are now staking your SOL!**
{% endhint %}

If you click on your stake accounts you will see that your stake is activating.&#x20;

On the Solana Network it takes 1 epoch for your stake to activate.&#x20;

An epoch in Solana lasts for approximately 2-3 days. After this period your stake will show up as active and will start earning rewards.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (68).png" alt="" width="563"><figcaption><p>Example of an activating Solana stake</p></figcaption></figure>

## How to Unstake SOL <a href="#h_01hc88s1agwrs5s2s0zgkjptz9" id="h_01hc88s1agwrs5s2s0zgkjptz9"></a>

If you click on your stake balance, you will be given the option to unstake.&#x20;

Unstaking also takes one epoch, which is about 2-3 days.&#x20;

Once you click Unstake your stake will start **deactivating** and will become fully **inactive** after a maximum of approximately 3 days (1 epoch).

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (69).png" alt="" width="352"><figcaption><p>Example of the unstaking screen</p></figcaption></figure>

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (70).png" alt="" width="364"><figcaption><p>Example of the staking interface in Phantom</p></figcaption></figure>

{% hint style="info" %}
After the stake become inactive you can withdraw it back to your Solana account.
{% endhint %}

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (71).png" alt="" width="362"><figcaption><p>Inactive Solana stakes ready to be withdrawn</p></figcaption></figure>

Click on one of your inactive stakes to view the details of it.&#x20;

From there, you can click the 3 horizontal dots in the upper right hand corner to withdraw your stake.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (72).png" alt="" width="365"><figcaption><p>Example of withdrawing a Solana stake.</p></figcaption></figure>

{% hint style="success" %}
Simply confirm the transaction in your wallet and you're all set!&#x20;

You've now successfully unstaked your Solana (SOL)&#x20;
{% endhint %}

## How to Partially Unstake SOL

Solana (SOL) can be partially unstaked if you wish to only unstake a portion of your total delegation.&#x20;

* This is done via a `split stake` transaction.&#x20;

To do so requires the use of the [Solflare wallet](https://solflare.com/onboard).

* If you need to set up a Solflare wallet, please see: [getting-started-solflare-wallet.md](../../wallets/wallets/getting-started-solflare-wallet.md "mention")

{% hint style="warning" %}
**Note:** In order to do this, it will require using a self-custody solution for your SOL, such as a Ledger device.

If you are using a custodian, please check in with them to see if a Solana split stake transaction is supported.&#x20;
{% endhint %}

You can access the Solflare wallet interface from this link, and from there connect your Ledger or other supported hardware wallet.

* [https://solflare.com/onboard](https://solflare.com/onboard)

If you are using a supported hardware wallet or wish to import the seed phrase from an existing wallet, select the option "I already a wallet".

{% hint style="danger" %}
If you choose to import a seed phrase, please be careful in doing so. You seed phrase should never be exposed or shared with others as it controls all the funds of your wallet.

Please take the proper precautions when entering it to restore a your wallet in Solflare.
{% endhint %}

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-10-02 at 10.40.57 AM (1).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

To connect your Ledger device, please make sure the firmware is up to date as well as the Solana app. Connect your Ledger device and open the Solana app to connect with Solflare.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-10-02 at 10.44.32 AM.png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

Follow the prompts to begin using the Solflare wallet controlled via your Ledger.&#x20;

Once you have done so, please follow the guide from Solflare on how to initiate a split stake transaction which will allow you to split your Solana stake into two (or more) accounts.&#x20;

* By doing so, you can then choose to unstake the account with the amount of SOL you wish to withdraw from staking.

The Solflare instructions can be found here under the section "Partial Unstaking".

* [Unstaking Your SOL on Solflare: A Guide with Options](https://help.solflare.com/en/articles/9264092-unstaking-your-sol-on-solflare-a-guide-with-options)
