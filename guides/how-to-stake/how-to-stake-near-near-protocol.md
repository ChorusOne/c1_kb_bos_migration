## About NEAR <a href="#h_01hc871mrar5z65205pjcp9q3f" id="h_01hc871mrar5z65205pjcp9q3f"></a>

NEAR is a decentralized blockchain designed to make it easier for developers to build and deploy decentralized applications (dApps). It focuses on scalability, developer-friendly tools, and user experience. NEAR uses a unique sharding mechanism called "Nightshade," which allows the network to process transactions in parallel, increasing throughput and reducing congestion.&#x20;

The blockchain operates on a Proof of Stake (PoS) consensus model, which is more energy-efficient compared to Proof of Work (PoW) and allows token holders to participate in securing the network through staking.

NEAR also emphasizes accessibility, offering features like human-readable account names instead of cryptographic addresses, and its innovative "Rainbow Bridge" enables seamless interoperability between NEAR and Ethereum, allowing assets and data to move across both networks.&#x20;

## How to Stake <a href="#h_01hc871mrar5z65205pjcp9q3f" id="h_01hc871mrar5z65205pjcp9q3f"></a>

### 1. Create a Wallet <a href="#h_01hc871mrawqv6cq019edyahyk" id="h_01hc871mrawqv6cq019edyahyk"></a>

To get started, you will want to choose a wallet. Some recommended wallets are [My Near Wallet](https://www.mynearwallet.com/) or [Meteor Wallet](https://wallet.meteorwallet.app/wallet).&#x20;

{% hint style="info" %}
This guide will be focusing on using [My Near Wallet](https://www.mynearwallet.com/).&#x20;

While both are suitable options, [Meteor Wallet](https://wallet.meteorwallet.app/wallet) may be a good choice if you are using a Ledger and holding NEAR already.&#x20;
{% endhint %}

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-09-06 at 5.03.40 PM.png" alt="" width="563"><figcaption><p>Landing page of My Near Wallet.</p></figcaption></figure>

Assuming you are planning on creating an account, you will want to select the blue box on the left, '**Create Account'**.

The screen below is what will pop up. Enter a strong and memorable password (or use a password manager) and click the consent boxes to continue.&#x20;

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-09-06 at 5.05.23 PM.png" alt="" width="375"><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

This is where you have the opportunity to create a NEAR Account. You pick your account name and then click Create Account.

* Note: You cannot change your account name once you have claimed it (although you can buy and sell it).

{% hint style="info" %}
Alternatively, if you already have a NEAR account or are using a Ledger device, select '**Import Existing Account**'.
{% endhint %}

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-09-06 at 5.06.36 PM.png" alt="" width="375"><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

In the next step, you need to choose your security method. You can opt for creating a new seed phrase, or creating a new account connected to your Ledger device.&#x20;

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-09-06 at 5.09.14 PM.png" alt="" width="375"><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

If you opt for the passphrase route (aka your wallet seed phrase) after you click continue you are shown the passphrase. It should be 12 words long.

{% hint style="danger" %}
Please be sure to store the mnemonic (passphrase) very securely.&#x20;

Anyone with the phrase can access your account and access your funds.&#x20;

* Please note the words should be written down exactly as shown, in order, and all lower case.&#x20;
* It is not advisable to store the passphrase digitally (i.e. a screenshot or text file) written down on paper and stored securely is ideal.&#x20;
{% endhint %}

To make sure you have recorded the mnemonic passphrase correctly, you will be prompted to input one of the words at random.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-09-06 at 5.13.16 PM.png" alt="" width="375"><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

After you do that, you will be brought to wallet home screen, as shown below.&#x20;

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-09-06 at 5.13.34 PM.png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

{% hint style="success" %}
Congratulations, your NEAR wallet is now setup!&#x20;

* Be sure to bookmark the website [https://app.mynearwallet.com/](https://app.mynearwallet.com/) to be sure you can readily access this wallet in the future.&#x20;
{% endhint %}

***

### 2. Fund your account to get Near ID <a href="#h_01hc871mraffcgc5szqat3rdf3" id="h_01hc871mraffcgc5szqat3rdf3"></a>

Here is where things can get a little tricky. NEAR is built around an Account model, where users have human-readable ID’s.&#x20;

In order to get that ID though, you need to fund your account with 0.1 NEAR.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-09-06 at 5.18.53 PM.png" alt="" width="375"><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

Clicking on either '**Receive**' or '**Deposit NEAR**' will present you with either and address to deposit NEAR to in order to fund your account, or show you different options for acquiring NEAR if you do not yet have any.&#x20;

Once you have some NEAR or have someone who can send you some, have it sent to your single use deposit address.&#x20;

* This can be found by clicking '**Receive**' as shown in the screenshot above.&#x20;
* Below is an example of how it would look.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-09-06 at 5.22.16 PM.png" alt="" width="375"><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

{% hint style="warning" %}
In order to create your new NEAR account, 0.05186 NEAR will be required at a minimum to be sent to create the account.&#x20;
{% endhint %}

You may need to refresh the wallet web page or navigate away from the screen and come back to it in order to get your NEAR balance to show.&#x20;

Once you receive the NEAR you're all set!  Congratulations your account is funded and that account ID is yours forever! (or until you decide to sell it)

### 3. Setting up your Account <a href="#h_01hc871mrav6gw9a6wf9aqx0yh" id="h_01hc871mrav6gw9a6wf9aqx0yh"></a>

Now that your account is funded you click on the 'Account' tab along the top of the page to make some changes (if you wish) to your account settings. &#x20;

On the right column, you can see your different security permissions. You can enable or disable password protection for your wallet, connect a Ledger hardware wallet, view your seed phrase, or name and export full access keys to your account.&#x20;

{% hint style="warning" %}
**Note:** It is possible to have multiple NEAR access keys all under your same wallet.&#x20;

The screenshot below highlights some of the options from the account tab, including a demonstration of a NEAR account controlled by both the seed phrase and from a Ledger device.&#x20;

_**However, it is recommended to choose one or the other to reduce possible points of failure.**_

Adding a Ledger to secure you existing NEAR account is best used if you later want to control this account with your Ledger device.&#x20;

* If you already have a funded Ledger device, please see: [Add or Import an Existing Account](how-to-stake-near-near-protocol.md#h_01hc871mraqftqgjaf2m19hrs1)
{% endhint %}



<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-09-19 at 3.40.07 PM.png" alt=""><figcaption><p>Example of the NEAR account management screen.</p></figcaption></figure>

You can also change your password here, delete or add access keys, or export your private key locally.&#x20;

Finally, if you wish to, you can remove your NEAR account from your wallet, although this is generally not advisable unless you have a specific reason for doing so.&#x20;

### 4. Add or Import an Existing Account <a href="#h_01hc871mraqftqgjaf2m19hrs1" id="h_01hc871mraqftqgjaf2m19hrs1"></a>

You might want to connect other existing NEAR accounts you have set up to the wallet interface. This will allow you to navigate quickly between accounts.&#x20;

From the home page of the wallet, in the top right corner you can either click import or create new account.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-09-19 at 3.51.08 PM.png" alt="" width="563"><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

If you want to import an existing account you will have many options to choose from, including a passphrase (aka seed phrase), using a Ledger device, importing a private key, or if you set up a NEAR account when these features were still offered, via an email or mobile number.&#x20;

{% hint style="info" %}
If you have a Ledger device that **already has a funded** NEAR account, this would be your best option.&#x20;
{% endhint %}

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-09-19 at 3.52.25 PM.png" alt="" width="563"><figcaption><p>Options available to import an existing NEAR account.</p></figcaption></figure>

{% hint style="success" %}
You are now set up with your NEAR account(s) and ready to stake!
{% endhint %}

### 5. Staking NEAR tokens <a href="#h_01hc871mransww277jn0ppps4n" id="h_01hc871mransww277jn0ppps4n"></a>

To stake your NEAR, navigate to the 'Staking' tab on the top left of the screen.&#x20;

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-09-19 at 4.02.29 PM.png" alt="" width="563"><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

Click on it and you will get a screen as displayed below.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-09-19 at 4.07.35 PM.png" alt="" width="461"><figcaption><p>Example of the NEAR staking interface.</p></figcaption></figure>

This is the first step in staking. Click the blue button 'Stake My Tokens' and you will next be prompted to select a validator.&#x20;

You can either scroll through the list of type `chorus` in the search bar to find the Chorus One public validator.&#x20;

* [chorusone.poolv1.near](https://nearblocks.io/address/chorusone.poolv1.near)

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-09-19 at 4.09.23 PM.png" alt="" width="476"><figcaption><p>Example of using the validator search function. </p></figcaption></figure>



Once you've selected the Chorus One validator, you will see an overview screen of your existing staking history (if applicable) as well as information about the validator.&#x20;

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-09-19 at 4.11.11 PM.png" alt="" width="452"><figcaption><p>The chorusone.poolv1.near validator.</p></figcaption></figure>

To begin, click 'Stake with Validator' and you will be prompted to input the amount of NEAR that you would like to stake.&#x20;

If you want to stake everything in your wallet click 'Use Max’ -- However, it is advisable to leave a small amount of NEAR in your wallet to cover future gas fees.&#x20;

Next, go ahead and click 'Submit Stake'.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-09-19 at 4.14.29 PM.png" alt="" width="443"><figcaption><p>The NEAR staking screen.</p></figcaption></figure>

Next, confirm your stake as depicted in the screenshot below. All you have to do is click ‘Confirm’.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-09-19 at 4.15.37 PM.png" alt="" width="462"><figcaption><p>Example of the staking confirmation screen.</p></figcaption></figure>

You will see the following screen upon successful staking!

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-09-19 at 4.18.04 PM.png" alt="" width="454"><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

{% hint style="success" %}
Congratulations you are now staking on NEAR Protocol!&#x20;

You can check in on updates and rewards from the Staking tab of the wallet.
{% endhint %}

### 6. Withdrawing Rewards (Unstaking) <a href="#h_01hc871mrapqhp1yf88hj33jfy" id="h_01hc871mrapqhp1yf88hj33jfy"></a>

Your account will start earning rewards upon staking and after some time you will be able to withdraw them.&#x20;

{% hint style="info" %}
Your earned rewards are automatically restaked.
{% endhint %}

If you want to unstake, you have to select the ‘Unstake’ on the right hand side from the Unstaking tab.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-09-19 at 4.34.06 PM (1).png" alt="" width="460"><figcaption><p>How to unstake your NEAR.</p></figcaption></figure>

This will then prompt you to select which validator you would like to unstake from.&#x20;

Unless you are staking through multiple different validators there should only be one option.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-09-19 at 4.36.44 PM.png" alt="" width="443"><figcaption><p>Here you will see which validators you are staking with. </p></figcaption></figure>

The following screen will then prompt you to input the amount of NEAR you wish to unstake.&#x20;

You can then choose to either unstake just your earned rewards or more if you like.

* Similar to the staking interface used before, you can also select 'Use Max'.&#x20;

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-09-19 at 4.37.32 PM.png" alt="" width="442"><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

Finally, confirm that you are ready to unstake that amount of NEAR that you entered.

The tokens become available on the 4th epoch after release which is usually between 52–65 hours in time.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-09-19 at 4.38.46 PM.png" alt="" width="452"><figcaption><p>Example of a pending NEAR unstaking transaction. </p></figcaption></figure>

After hitting confirm you will then get the following message showing you successfully unstaked your NEAR.&#x20;

{% hint style="info" %}
Note: Unstaking takes 4 epochs, which is roughly 52-65 hours.&#x20;
{% endhint %}

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-09-19 at 4.39.57 PM.png" alt="" width="474"><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

To check on your unstaking progress, navigate to the Staking tab of your wallet, which will reflect your tokens pending release and eventually they will become available for you to withdraw.&#x20;

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-09-19 at 4.42.18 PM.png" alt="" width="474"><figcaption><p>Example of a pending NEAR balance after unstaking. </p></figcaption></figure>

After the required 4 epochs have passed, you can return to the wallet and you will see your NEAR ready to unstake.&#x20;

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/NEAR Unstaking 1.png" alt="" width="461"><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

Simply click on 'Withdraw' and you will be prompted to select the validator you wish to withdraw from.&#x20;

* Most likely this will only be one option unless you have initiated an unstaking transaction from multiple validators.&#x20;

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/NEAR Unstaking 4.png" alt="" width="461"><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/NEAR Unstaking 3.png" alt="" width="472"><figcaption><p>Example of the final withdraw confirmation screen.</p></figcaption></figure>

{% hint style="success" %}
Simply finalize and approve the transaction and you're all set!&#x20;

You've successfully withdrawn your NEAR and the balance will appear back in your wallet.&#x20;
{% endhint %}
